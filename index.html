Self Introduction

I am a fifth-year Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Economics at the University of Nevada, Reno (UNR), currently on the job market for the 2025–2026 academic year.

My research fields are Labor Economics, Health Economics, Public Economics, and Applied Microeconomics. Broadly, my research examines how public policies and individual factors shape economic and health outcomes, with a particular focus on vulnerable populations.

❓ What drives self-employment? Is it primarily dominated and driven by opportunity or by necessity in different contexts?

❓ To what extent can policies designed to support households effectively enhance social welfare?
